How to Play Chefle

Chefle is a daily food guessing game. Your goal is to identify the mystery dish by comparing each guess against a small set of culinary clues. The puzzle is built to reward food intuition: think about what a dish is usually made from, where it is commonly associated, whether it is hot or cold, and what kind of eating experience it has.

The Daily Puzzle

Each day has one answer. Everyone playing the same daily mode sees the same target dish for that day. The answer is chosen from a rotating pool of familiar dishes, so the challenge is not about memorizing one recipe card. It is about narrowing down broad food patterns.

Making a Guess

  1. Type a dish name into the search box or pick one from the daily pool list.
  2. Submit the guess to reveal a row of clue tiles.
  3. Green tiles mean your guess matches the target for that clue.
  4. Red tiles mean the clue does not match. Number clues may show an up or down hint.
  5. Use the remaining choices to make a better next guess.

Reading the Clues

The clue categories are intentionally broad. A dish might be grouped by a large world region, a primary protein, a base starch, serving temperature, calories, texture, or prep/cook estimate. Those clues are simplified so players can reason from normal food knowledge instead of exact restaurant nutrition facts.

Useful Guessing Strategy

Start with a dish that covers common categories, such as a hot poultry dish with a bread or rice base. After the first row, pay attention to the strongest separators: region, protein, base carb, and temperature. If calories point upward, try a richer dish; if they point downward, move toward lighter meals, salads, soups, or small plates.

Why Estimates Are Used

Real dishes vary by recipe, restaurant, serving size, and country. A burger, curry, pasta, or salad can be prepared many ways. Chefle uses editorial estimates so the game has stable clues and a fair daily answer. The estimates are not nutrition, allergy, or medical advice.

Daily and Practice Play

The daily game is the official score for sharing. Practice or infinite play is there for players who want more puzzles after finishing the day. Practice results do not change the official daily outcome.
