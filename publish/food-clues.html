Chefle Food Clue Guide

Chefle clues are designed around everyday food recognition. The archive does not try to settle the one true version of a dish. Instead, each dish is assigned a practical game profile that helps players compare one meal idea against another.

Cuisine Region

Region clues describe broad culinary association, not ownership. A dish can travel across countries and change over time. Chefle uses region as a helpful sorting clue, grouping dishes into familiar buckets such as North American, South American, European, Asian, African, and Oceanic when appropriate.

Primary Protein

The protein clue points to the main protein identity a player is likely to notice. Some dishes are clear, such as beef stew or roast chicken. Others are grouped as egg, dairy, legume, plant-based, seafood, or no primary protein when the dish is better understood by starch, vegetables, sauce, or dessert structure.

Base Carb

The base carb clue covers the main starch or structural base. Bread, wraps, pastry, batter, rice, grains, pasta, noodles, potatoes, corn, beans, produce, nuts, and no-starch categories help distinguish dishes that otherwise share protein or region.

Serving Temperature

Temperature is a simple clue: hot, cold, or room temperature. It describes the most common serving style in the game archive. Some dishes can be eaten more than one way, but Chefle chooses the version most players would expect first.

Calories

Calories are rounded gameplay estimates. A green match means the estimate is the same as the target's estimate. An upward hint means the target is estimated higher; a downward hint means the target is estimated lower. The clue is meant to separate light bites from richer meals, not to provide diet advice.

Texture

Texture clues describe the eating experience: crispy, creamy, saucy, brothy, chewy, tender, crunchy, flaky, and similar broad impressions. Texture helps make the puzzle feel more culinary than purely nutritional.

Prep and Cook Estimate

Prep/cook estimates group dishes by typical effort. A slow stew, roasted main, quick sandwich, chilled salad, or baked dessert tends to fall into different time bands. These estimates are simplified because actual cooking time changes with equipment, recipe, and batch size.

Editorial Review

When a clue feels misleading, Chefle treats that as a data-quality issue. The goal is not to be perfectly academic; the goal is to make each puzzle row teach the next guess. Clear player feedback can lead to clue adjustments in later versions of the archive.
